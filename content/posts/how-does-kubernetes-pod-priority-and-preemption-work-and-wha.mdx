> **Understanding How does Kubernetes Pod Priority and Preemption work, and what happens when a higher-priority pod cannot be scheduled due to resource constraints?**

## Overview

Pod Priority determines the scheduling order of pods—higher numeric values indicate greater importance. Preemption allows higher-priority pods to evict lower-priority ones. When no node has sufficient resources to fit a high-priority pod, the scheduler may evict lower-priority pods to free up resources. If eviction is not possible, the high-priority pod remains in a pending state until resources become available or new nodes are added to the cluster. When preemption occurs, the scheduler considers PodDisruptionBudgets (PDBs) to ensure minimum availability for protected workloads, and it respects graceful termination periods before forcibly killing evicted pods.
