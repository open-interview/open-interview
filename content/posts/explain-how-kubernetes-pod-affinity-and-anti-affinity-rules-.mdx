> **Understanding Explain how Kubernetes pod affinity and anti-affinity rules work, and describe a scenario where using pod anti-affinity would be critical for application high availability.**

## Overview

Pod affinity allows pods to be co-located on the same node or topology domain, while anti-affinity ensures pods are distributed across nodes, availability zones, or regions. Pod anti-affinity is essential for high availability because it eliminates single points of failure—if one node or zone fails, pods governed by anti-affinity rules will continue running on remaining infrastructure.
