> **Picture this:** It was 3am when Capitec Bank's fraud defense lit up, testing cross-account risk at scale. Capitec faced 3.5M+ daily fraud cases and leaned on a graph-powered scoring pipeline to expose illicit links hiding beyond transaction signals [1]. This real-world pressure becomes a blueprint for building an on-device, privacy-preserving, explainable system that works across devices—from low-end mobiles to desktops. The journey that follows translates that urgency into a practical, deployable design.

## Chapter 1 — Capitec Wake-Up: Fraud Without Borders

- Capitec's surge exposed a fundamental truth: fraud travels across connected accounts and payment paths, not just single transactions. Capitec's graph-powered scoring pipeline demonstrated how cross-entity relationships reveal hidden networks at production scale [1].
- From this awakening, a two-tier architecture emerges: a tiny on-device model that runs at sub-25 ms to keep latency predictable, and a cloud ensemble that coordinates cross-device calibration and updates. This separation preserves responsiveness where users notice it most while enabling global learning from many devices.

## Chapter 2 — Edge-First Design: Device-Aware Models

- Device diversity drives model selection. High-end devices get powerful, compact models; mid-range devices get leaner architectures; low-end devices fall back to rule-based checks to ensure responsiveness and avoid outages.

```python
# Pseudo-model selector based on device specs
def select_model(cpu_mhz, mem_mb):
    if cpu_mhz > 1500 and mem_mb > 400:
        return 'distilled_bert'
    elif cpu_mhz > 800 and mem_mb > 200:
        return 'lightweight_cnn'
    else:
        return 'rule_based_fallback'
```

- Latency targets drive quantization and pruning. On-device inference must stay under 25 ms on mid-range CPUs [2], while the cloud ensemble handles cross-device calibration and updates [3].
- Practical takeaway: start with a tiered model catalog, then optimize per-tier with lightweight quantization and selective pruning to maintain responsiveness.

## Chapter 3 — Privacy by Design

- Features stored per-user with privacy budgets, ensuring local privacy accounting even as signals traverse devices [4].
- Updates use federated learning with secure aggregation, so raw data never leaves the device, while the central server coordinates model improvements without peeking at individual examples [5][6].
- Differential privacy budgets govern how much information can be disclosed in updates, enabling auditable privacy controls while preserving utility [7].
- Practical takeaway: pair per-user feature stores with federated updates and DP budgets to balance accuracy, privacy, and regulatory needs.

## Chapter 4 — Explainability and Regulation

- Regulatory explainability requires local explanations that can be audited without pulling data from devices. Local, device-level explanations help reviewers verify model rationale in high-stakes payments scenarios [8].
- Combine on-device explainability with a cloud-driven audit trail to meet both privacy and compliance demands while keeping throughput intact [9].
- Practical takeaway: design explainability that travels with inference, not with data exfiltration.

## Chapter 5 — Drift, Evaluation, and Rollout

- Concept drift is detected through continuous evaluation and rolling retraining, so models adapt to evolving fraud patterns without manual retraining on every bump in the road [10].
- Evaluation uses stratified A/B testing with latency budgets and explainability metrics to ensure fair comparisons across device cohorts [11].
- Rollout follows canary deployment with device-specific cohorts and automated rollback triggers for failures, reducing risk while expanding coverage [12].

## Chapter 6 — The Takeaways

- Build a two-tier architecture: on-device models for latency and a cloud ensemble for calibration.
- Prioritize privacy: per-user feature stores, federated updates, secure aggregation, and DP budgets.
- Ensure explainability: local, regulatory-friendly explanations with auditable trails.
- Plan for drift: continuous evaluation and rolling retraining with automated rollbacks.
- Roll out with canaries: device-specific cohorts to minimize impact if issues arise.
