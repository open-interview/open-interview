> **Understanding How would you configure Kubernetes to dedicate specific nodes to specialized workloads (e.g., GPU-accelerated pods) while preventing other workloads from being scheduled on those nodes, and what happens when a pod without the proper tolerations is submitted to the cluster?**

## Overview

Use node taints to mark nodes with specialized requirements and pod tolerations to allow matching pods to schedule on them. Pods without the appropriate tolerations will remain pending until a suitable node becomes available, or be rejected entirely if no such nodes exist.
